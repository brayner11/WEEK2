Week 2

This week was a learning journey packed with different topics. We started with understanding objects and the basics of HTML. I also got to know how databases work and had a useful employability session.

Day 1: We learned how to create our first web page using HTML, how to add images, how to create our first heading, our first paragraph, add lists, and even know how HTML pages are structured.

Day 2: On the second day, we had a session focused on employability, and the topic was LinkedIn. During this session, we learned valuable information about how to use and optimize our LinkedIn profiles for professional networking and career opportunities.

We also delved into the realm of relational databases, learning about the structure and organization of data. This provided insights into how to effectively manage and retrieve information in a structured manner, which is crucial for various applications in the field of data management and analysis.

Day 3: On the third day, we focused on HTML attributes, which are like special instructions that make our web pages look and behave the way we want. We also learned about Git and GitHub, tools that help us keep track of changes in our coding projects and work together smoothly.

To practice what we learned, we worked on a project and took on some coding challenges. It was a day full of hands-on experience, making our websites more interesting and learning how to collaborate better in coding.

Day 4 : On the fourth day, we explored SQL (Structured Query Language) and how to use it to ask specific questions or retrieve specific information from databases. This included learning about queries and filters, which are like search commands for databases.

The day also involved facing challenges related to SQL. These challenges provided practical scenarios where we could apply our newfound knowledge, reinforcing our skills in crafting effective queries and filters. It was a day dedicated to understanding and using databases more effectively through SQL.

Day 5 : On day five, we learned about CSS, a cool tool to make our websites look awesome. CSS helps us pick colors, arrange things neatly, and choose fonts for our web pages. It's like giving our websites a stylish makeover, making them not just work well but look great too!
